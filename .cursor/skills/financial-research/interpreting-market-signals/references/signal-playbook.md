# Signal Playbook

Detailed catalog of signal patterns, what they typically mean, and historical anchors. Use as a checklist against the day's analysis — most days trigger 2–4 of these patterns.

## 1. Divergences

| Divergence | Typical reading |
|------------|-----------------|
| Equities up, credit spreads widening | Credit markets pricing risk equities ignore — historically credit leads (2007, mid-2015) |
| Equities up, transports/small-caps lagging | Narrow leadership; rally breadth deteriorating |
| Copper falling, oil rising | Demand weakness + supply shock — stagflationary mix |
| Gold and real yields both rising | Safe-haven/official-sector demand overpowering the usual rate anchor (visible 2022–24 during central-bank gold buying) |
| A-shares up, H-shares flat/down | Domestic policy-driven optimism not shared by foreign investors (and vice versa) |
| CNY fix stable, CNH weakening | Offshore market testing PBOC resolve; watch for countermeasures (reserve-requirement moves on FX forwards) |
| Bond yields up, bank stocks down | Rising yields read as fiscal/inflation stress, not growth optimism |
| VIX low, single-stock volatility high | Index calm masking rotation churn; correlation breakdown |

## 2. Non-Reactions (positioning tells)

- **Strong earnings/data, market sells off** — good news fully priced; positioning stretched long. Common at cycle tops.
- **Bad news, market shrugs** — bearishness saturated; potential for squeeze rallies. (E.g., markets rallying through weak data when easing is expected: "bad news is good news" regime.)
- **Repeated stimulus announcements, diminishing market response** — policy credibility eroding; each subsequent measure needs to be bigger to move prices. Key China pattern 2023–24.

## 3. Communication Deltas

- Central bank drops/adds a single word ("patient", "transitory", "additional firming") — markets reprice entire paths on these.
- Chinese official language shifts (see `analyzing-china-macro`): 稳健→适度宽松 on monetary policy stance (Dec 2024) preceded the largest easing repricing in years.
- Officials denying something not yet publicly discussed ("no plans to devalue") — the denial reveals the internal discussion.
- Scheduled communication canceled or delayed — often precedes a policy surprise.

## 4. Second-Order Effects

Trace the supply chain / exposure chain of any first-order headline:

| First-order headline | Second-order checks |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| Chip export controls tightened | Semiconductor equipment makers, Chinese domestic-substitution names, memory pricing |
| Oil supply shock | Airlines/chemicals (cost), high-yield energy credit (benefit), inflation expectations, Asian importers' FX (INR, JPY) |
| Property developer default | Suppliers' receivables, trust products, local government land revenue, bank exposure disclosures |
| Major tariff announcement | Trans-shipment economies (Vietnam, Mexico), retailer margins, FX of targeted country, substitution suppliers |
| Bank failure/rescue | Deposit-flow beneficiaries, similar-balance-sheet peers, central bank liquidity facilities usage |

## 5. Coinciding Events (theme detection)

Three or more same-day items sharing an underlying driver = a theme worth naming:
- Multiple funding-stress items (repo spike + CP spread widening + a failed auction) → liquidity stress theme
- Multiple consumer-weakness items across regions → global demand theme
- Several countries' inflation surprising the same direction → common shock (energy, FX, supply chains), not local noise

## 6. Dogs That Didn't Bark

- Weak data with **no** policy response signal → authorities' tolerance band is wider than assumed, or they're constrained (FX, inflation)
- Expected intervention that didn't come (BOJ absent above a "line in the sand") → line has moved
- Routine liquidity operation skipped or shrunk → possible stance shift before any announcement
- Quarterly data release delayed without explanation (China GDP, Oct 2022) → treat the delay itself as information

## Calibration Notes

- Historical anchors above are prompts, not proofs. Name the mechanism in today's terms before citing the analogy.
- Base rates matter: divergences resolve without crisis far more often than they precede one. That's why grades exist — a fresh divergence alone is WEAK–MODERATE; a divergence plus a mechanism plus corroborating flow data can be STRONG.
